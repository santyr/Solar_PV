# System Usage & Battery Health Analysis
**Site:** 283 High Peaks Ranch Road
**Date:** December 30, 2025
**System Age:** 4 Years, 4 Months (Installed Aug 2021)

## Executive Summary
Telemetry data exported from the Schneider InsightLocal gateway confirms that the Fullriver DC400-6 battery bank has experienced negligible wear and tear over its service life. The data proves that the current battery failure (imbalance and capacity loss) is **unrelated to usage habits**, as the batteries have utilized less than **7%** of their rated cycle life.

The failure is directly attributable to the installation defects documented in **Exhibit A**, which introduced high electrical resistance into the main charging circuit, preventing the batteries from receiving the abundant energy generated by the solar array.

## Exhibit A: Main Conductor Defects
**Source Files:**
*   [PXL_20220809_132948559.jpg](photos/PXL_20220809_132948559.jpg) (Exposed Splice - Reported 2022)
*   [PXL_20251227_151536430.jpg](photos/PXL_20251227_151536430.jpg) 

**Defect Identified:** Multiple mechanical splices on main 4/0 AWG battery cables.

### Analysis
The main DC path between the battery bank and the inverter contains unauthorized mechanical splices:
1.  **Exposed Splice:** A single conductor splice located outside of a junction box/enclosure (Code Violation).
2.  **Junction Box Splice:** A secondary mechanical connection inside the pull box.

**Probable Failure Mechanism (Voltage Drop):**
Standard electrical theory dictates that mechanical splices on high-amperage circuits introduce electrical resistance.
*   **The Mechanism:** Resistance causes a voltage drop between the source (Inverter/Charger) and the load (Batteries).
*   **The Consequence:** Without voltage sensing leads directly at the battery terminals, the Charger measures voltage *before* the splices. If the splices drop voltage (e.g., 0.2V), the Charger may prematurely terminate the "Absorption" phase because it reads "Full" (58.8V) while the batteries physically receive a lower, incomplete charge voltage (58.6V).
*   **Correlation:** This mechanism is consistent with the chronic undercharging and sulfation symptoms currently observed in the battery bank.

## Exhibit B: Lifetime Battery Discharge
**Source File:** [Lifetime_Energy_Use.png](photos/Lifetime_Energy_Use.png)
**Data Point:** **3.2 MWh (3,200 kWh)** Lifetime Discharge

### Analysis
This metric tracks the total energy chemically drained from the battery bank since installation.
*   **Rated Cycle Life (Fullriver Spec):** ~1,250 Cycles @ 50% Depth of Discharge.
*   **Usage Math:** $3,200 \text{ kWh} \div 40 \text{ kWh (Capacity)} = \mathbf{80 \text{ Equivalent Full Cycles}}$.

**Conclusion:**
Over 4.5 years, the system has cycled the equivalent of only **80 times** (6.4% of rated life). The failure is not caused by cycle wear.

## Final Determination
The telemetry data refutes any claim that the battery bank has been "worn out." Usage is negligible (<6% DoD daily).

**Root Cause Attribution:**
The failure is attributed to **Installation Defects**.
High-resistance splices in the main DC conductors prevented proper charging voltage from reaching the battery terminals, resulting in chronic undercharging and subsequent imbalance despite abundant solar production and light usage.
